Raw interpolation

class actuarial.yield_curve.RawInterpolation[source]

Bases: actuarial.superclass.Piecewise.Piecewise, actuarial.superclass.TermStructure.TermStructure, actuarial.yield_curve.YieldCurve.YieldCurve

A raw interpolated yield curve assumes that the instantaneous forward rate is constant between two consecutive maturities.

Given a set of zero-coupon bonds price (discount factors) to interpolate \(\lbrace(\tau_i,P_i)\rbrace_{i=0,...,n}\) with initial conditions \(\tau_0=0\) and \(P_0=1\), the raw interpolated zero-coupon bond price for a maturity \(\tau \in [\tau_i,\tau_{i+1})\) is given by

\[P(\tau) = P_i e^{-\mu_i(\tau-\tau_i)}\]

where \(\mu_i\) is the constant instantaneous forward rate on the interval \([\tau_i,\tau_{i+1})\) given by

\[\mu_i = - \frac{\ln(P_{i+1}) - \ln(P_i)}{\tau_{i+1} - \tau_i}.\]

Note

Let \(\lbrace(\tau_i,P_i)\rbrace_{i=0,...,n}\) be a set of zero-coupon bonds price (discount factors) to interpolate. We assume that we have the initial condition \(\tau_0=0\) and \(P_0=1\).

The raw interpolated zero-coupon bond price at maturity \(\tau \in [\tau_i,\tau_{i+1})\) is given by

\[P(\tau) = P_i e^{-\mu_i(\tau-\tau_i)}\]

where \(\mu_i\) is the constant instantaneous forward rate on the interval \([\tau_i,\tau_{i+1})\) given by

\[\mu_i = - \frac{\ln(P_{i+1}) - \ln(P_i)}{\tau_{i+1} - \tau_i}.\]

Note that above the maximum maturity \(\tau_n\), an extrapolation method is used. The extrapolation assumes that the instantaneous forward rate \(\mu_{n-1}\) calculated for the interval \([\tau_{n-1},\tau_n)\) continues to apply above \(\tau_n\).

As the raw interpolation is performed on discount factors, the algorithm first converts the yield curve to interpolate to discount factors (e.g., if compound rates are provided, they are converted to discount factors), then peforms the interpolation on these discount factors as described in the formula above and finally converts the interpolated discount factors to the same type as the yield curve to interpolate (e.g., the interpolated discount factors are converted to compound rates).
