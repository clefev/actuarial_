Price index

class actuarial.indexation.PriceIndex[source]

Bases: actuarial.superclass.Piecewise.Piecewise, actuarial.superclass.TermStructure.TermStructure, actuarial.indexation.Indexation.Indexation

A price index is a measure of how prices change over a period of time, or in other words, it is a way to measure inflation.

We denote \(PI_i\) the value of the price index observed at maturity \(\tau_i\). Between two consecutive maturities \(\tau_i\) and \(\tau_{i+1}\), the value of the price index is interpolated.

price_index(maturities)[source]

Evaluate the price index for the given maturities.

Note

Depending on the type of interpolation, the price index at maturity \(\tau \in [\tau_i,\tau_{i+1})\) is calculated as follows:

  • Lower interpolation:

    \[PI(\tau) = PI_i\]

  • Linear interpolation:

    \[PI(\tau) = \alpha(\tau) PI_i+ (1 - \alpha(\tau)) PI_{i+1}\]

  • Exponential interpolation:

    \[PI(\tau) = PI_i e^{\beta_i (\tau - \tau_i)}\]

where \(\alpha(\tau) = \frac{\tau_{i+1} - \tau}{\tau_{i+1} - \tau_i}\) and \(\beta_i = \frac{\ln(PI_{i+1}) - \ln(PI_i)}{\tau_{i+1} - \tau_i}\).

discount_factor([maturities1=0, ]maturities2)

Compute the inflation discount factor between two maturities.

Note

The inflation discount factor between maturities \(\tau_1\) and \(\tau_2\) is given by

\[\frac{PI(\tau_1)}{PI(\tau_2)}\]

where \(PI(\tau)\) is the price index at maturity \(\tau\).

The discount factor can be used for price adjustment purpose. It provides the price at \(\tau_1\) (taking into account the inflation) of something originally purchased at \(\tau_2\) with a unit cost.

The discount factor is the mathematical inverse of the capitalization factor.

Parameters

  • maturities1 (array-like) – Starting maturities \(\tau_1\). The default is 0.

  • maturities2 (array-like) – Ending maturities \(\tau_2\).

capitalization_factor([maturities1=0, ]maturities2)

Compute the inflation capitalization factor between two maturities.

Note

The inflation capitalization factor between maturities \(\tau_1\) and \(\tau_2\) is given by

\[\frac{PI(\tau_2)}{PI(\tau_1)}\]

where \(PI(\tau)\) is the price index at maturity \(\tau\).

The capitalization factor can be used for price adjustment purpose. It provides the price at \(\tau_2\) (taking into account the inflation) of something originally purchased at \(\tau_1\) with a unit cost.

The capitalization factor is the mathematical inverse of the discount factor.

inflation([maturities1=0, ]maturities2)

Compute the rate of inflation between two maturities.

Note

The rate of inflation between maturities \(\tau_1\) and \(\tau_2\) is given by

\[\frac{PI(\tau_2)}{PI(\tau_1)} - 1\]

where \(PI(\tau)\) is the price index at maturity \(\tau\).

The rate of inflation corresponds to the capitalization factor minus one.

normalize(maturity=0, base=100)[source]

Normalize in-place the price index. The value of the price index at the provided reference maturity will be equal to the base value.

Note

The new normalized price index at any maturity \(\tau_i\) is calculated as follows

\[PI^{normalized}(\tau_i) = PI(\tau_i) \frac{base}{PI(\tau^*)}\]

where \(PI(\tau)\) is the price index before normalization and \(base\) is the provided base value to reach at the provided reference maturity \(\tau^*\).

In this way, we have

\[PI^{normalized}(\tau^*) = base.\]
Parameters

  • maturity (scalar, optional) – Reference maturity. The default is 0.

  • base (scalar, optional) – Base value. The default is 100.
